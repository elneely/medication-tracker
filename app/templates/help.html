Medication Questions

Adding a medication

Once you have navigated to the {% if user %}Add Medication{% else %}Add Medication{% endif %} page you will find a form where you can enter a great deal of information about your medication. If it seems overwhelming, just remember that the only required information for a medication is its name. Anything else is optional!

So what information can you store on the form?

Name: The only required field on this form, this is for the medication's name. Note that this does not have to be unique, in case you have two different dosages of the same medication for some reason, say. However, if you have two medications with the same name, you might want to note the difference between them somewhere.

Brand name: Sometimes generic medication names are long and hard to spell or pronounce. If you are in this situation, you might want to add a brand name, which is usually shorter and easier to remember. This is also helpful if the medical staff is inconsistent about which name they use; you won't be stuck trying to remember that Synthroid is Levothyroxine.

Dose: What is the dosage of your medication? e.g., 500 mg, .75 mcg, 5mL, 2 teaspoons, etc.

Frequency: How often do you need to take this medication? e.g., Twice a day, Once at bedtime, Once every 7 days, etc.

Length: How long is this prescription? If you are on a long-term medication (sometimes called a maintenance medication) this could be 90 days. If not, it can range anywhere from 30 days to 1 day (although you probably don't need to enter it in the medication tracker if you only take it for one day, unless you really want to!)

Short-term medication?: On your user page your medications are divided into short-term and long-term medications. This checkbox allows you to specify a medication is short-term. If it is not checked, the medication is assumed to be long-term.

Reason: It is easy to lose track of what medication you are taking for what purpose. This allows you to make a little note to remind yourself of why you are taking this medication.

Add Doctor: This allows you to record what doctor has prescribed your medication, which can be handy to know if you have run out of refills! You can either select a doctor you have previously used or you can add a new doctor. New doctors will automatically be added to your Doctor List. If you change your mind about adding a doctor, just press the Don't Add Doctor button!

Add Pharmacy: This allows you to keep track of what pharmacy you filled your prescription at. Just as for adding a doctor, you can select a pharmacy you have already used or you can add a new one; new ones will be added to your Pharmacy List. If you change your mind about adding a pharmacy, press the Don't Add Pharmacy button and it will go away!

Prescription Date: This is the date your doctor prescribed your medication. Not every medication label includes this information, but you can record it here if they do.

Last Filled: This helps you keep track of when you most recently filled this prescription. Note that if you don't keep this up to date, your refill reminders will stop being helpful!

Prescription Expiration Date: Prescriptions don't last forever, so this helps you keep track of when your prescription or refills expire. Sometimes your medication label will also include when the medication itself expires (i.e., when it is past the date you should be using it.) For most of us, the expiration date of the prescription is more useful to keep track of, since you can't refill a prescription that has expired.

Number of refills remaining: A crucial piece of information for long-term medications, here you can keep track of how many refills you have left.

Refill reminders on?: If you would like to be reminded of when you need to refill this medication, simply check this box and enter how many days in advance you wish to be reminded. (3 days before you run out? 7 days?) If you do wish to be reminded, you will also need to enter when the medication was last filled, how long the prescription is for, and how many days in advance you want to be reminded. e.g., if your prescription is 30 days long and you want to be reminded 4 days before you run out, then your reminders will start 26 days after you last filled it.

Notes: This is for anything else you wish to record about this medication. Do you tend to have side-effects from it? Do you need to take it with food or wait 30 minutes before you eat? Anything is fair game for this field!
